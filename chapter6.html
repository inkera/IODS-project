Figure 1 plots the individual response profiles of by treatment group for the rats data. First thing to notice are the clear level differences between the groups: group 2 is slightly below in level terms to group 3 at all values of Time-variable, where as group 1 is significantly below groups 2 and 3 with a difference 300 points from the max values of group 1 vs minimum of group 2.

All groups seam to have relatively similar slopes among individuals, with perhaps a small exception in Group 2, which speaks of tracking phenomenon i.e.Â individuals having high scores in the beginning tend to have them in the end as well.

Variability in the groups looks quite small with all groups having one possible outlier with Group 2 having one large outlier. Slopes are also increasing i.e.Â rats scores increase over time.

On the BPRS data based on Kimmoâs book:

Here 40 male subjects were randomly assigned to one of two treatment groups and each subject was rated on the brief psychiatric rating scale (BPRS) measured before treatment began (week 0) and then at weekly intervals for eight weeks. The BPRS assesses the level of 18 symptom constructs such as hostility, suspiciousness, hallucinations and grandiosity; each of these is rated from one (not present) to seven (extremely severe). The scale is used to evaluate patients suspected of having schizophrenia.
